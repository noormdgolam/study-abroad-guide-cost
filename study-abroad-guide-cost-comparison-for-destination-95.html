Academic Fees

Average annual tuition fees for international students at universities in Destination 95 range from $10,000 to $20,000. Some programs may have additional fees for things like materials, equipment, or field trips.

Health Insurance

Students are required to have health insurance that covers them for the duration of their studies. The cost of health insurance varies depending on the provider and the level of coverage chosen, but it typically ranges from $500 to $1,000 per year.

Scholarships and Financial Aid

Destination 95 offers a range of scholarships and financial aid options to help international students cover the costs of their studies. These scholarships can cover up to 50% of tuition fees and living expenses, and some may also provide a stipend for living expenses.

Taxes and Refund Process

Students from Destination 95 will be required to pay taxes on their income earned while studying abroad. It's essential to understand the tax implications of studying abroad and to seek advice from a tax professional to ensure compliance with all tax requirements.

Financial Assistance Resources

International students can access a range of financial assistance resources to help them cover the costs of studying abroad, including:

  • Student loans: Students can borrow from a range of lenders to cover the costs of their tuition fees and living expenses.
  • Grants: Many organizations and governments offer grants to students who meet specific eligibility criteria, such as low-income students or students from underrepresented groups.
  • Sponsorship: Some students may receive sponsorship from their government or employer to cover the costs of their studies.

Destination 95's student services team is available to provide guidance and support to international students on all aspects of studying abroad, including financial assistance and navigating the tax implications of being a student abroad.
