Cost Comparison for Destination 70

Last Updated: January 2022

When considering studying abroad in Destination 70, it's essential to have a clear understanding of the associated costs. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the expenses you may incur during your study abroad experience.

  • Living Expenses:
    • Accommodation:
      • A shared apartment in the city center: $600 - $800 per month
      • A shared apartment outside the city center: $400 - $600 per month
      • A private apartment in the city center: $1,000 - $1,200 per month
      • A private apartment outside the city center: $700 - $900 per month
    • Food:
      • Eating out: $15 - $25 per meal
      • Groceries: $50 - $75 per week
    • Transportation:
      • Public transportation: $1.50 - $3.00 per ride
      • Monthly public transportation pass: $60 - $100
    • Utilities:
      • Electricity, water, and internet: $100 - $150 per month
    • Health Insurance:
      • An international student health insurance plan: $500 - $800 per year
  • Tuition Fees:

    Tuition fees for Destination 70 vary depending on the institution and the program. Here are some estimated costs:

    • Undergraduate program: $10,000 - $20,000 per year
    • Graduate program: $15,000 - $30,000 per year
  • Other Expenses:

    Additional costs to consider include:

    • Books and supplies: $500 - $1,000 per semester
    • Student visa application fees: $100 - $200
    • Health checks and vaccinations: $200 - $500
    • Language courses: $500 - $1,000 per semester

Overall, a typical study abroad budget for Destination 70 could range from $20,000 to $40,000 per year, depending on your lifestyle and personal preferences.
