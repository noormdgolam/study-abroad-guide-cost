Study Abroad Guide: Cost Comparison for Destination 56

Published: July 15, 2026 • Written by Editorial Team

Destination 56, with its rich cultural heritage and world-class educational institutions, makes it a coveted destination for international students. To help navigate the complexities of studying abroad, we provide a comprehensive guide to the Cost Comparison for Destination 56.

Living Expenses

Living costs in Destination 56 are relatively high, with the cost of accommodation being the primary expense. Here's a general breakdown of estimated living expenses per month:

  • Accommodation: $1,200 - $2,000
  • Food and Groceries: $600 - $900
  • Transportation: $100 - $200
  • Utilities and Bills: $150 - $250
  • Health Insurance: $150 - $300
  • Social Expenses (Dining out, Movies, Entertainment): $500 - $800

The estimated monthly living expense for a student in Destination 56 can range between $2,800 and $4,950. These costs are subject to change and may vary based on individual circumstances.

Tuition Fees

Destination 56, as a popular international student destination, offers various degree programs at different price points. Here are some general tuition fee estimates broken down by level of study:

  • Undergraduate Programs: $10,000 - $25,000 per annum
  • Postgraduate Programs: $15,000 - $35,000 per annum
  • Certificate and Diploma Programs: $5,000 - $15,000 per annum

These estimates are subject to change and may vary depending on the institution, program of study, and course load.

Additional Costs

In addition to living expenses and tuition fees, students should also budget for the following:

  • VISA Application: $150 - $300
  • Student ID Card and Registration Fees: $100 - $200
  • Medical and Insurance Expenses: $500 - $1,000
  • Books, Stationery, and Technology: $500 - $1,000
  • Cultural and Social Activities: $500 - $1,000
